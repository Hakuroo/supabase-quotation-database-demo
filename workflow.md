# Quotation Workflow

This workflow shows how a centralized quotation database can support a practical construction and industrial budget calculation process.

## 1. Define Project Scope

**Goal**  
Understand what needs to be quoted before selecting materials, labor, services, or equipment.

**Inputs**

- Project type
- Site or installation requirements
- Expected deliverables
- Timeline or delivery constraints
- Internal assumptions

**Output**

- Clear quote scope
- Initial assumptions
- Questions for manual review

**Why it reduces repetitive manual work**
A consistent scope structure helps the team avoid rebuilding the same intake notes for every new quotation.

## 2. Select Materials

**Goal**  
Choose the physical materials required for the project.

**Inputs**

- Material catalog
- Units of measurement
- Fictional unit costs
- Quantity estimates

**Output**

- Material line items
- Materials subtotal

**Why it reduces repetitive manual work**
Centralized material records reduce repeated lookup tasks and help keep units and costs consistent.

## 3. Add Labor/Service Items by Category

**Goal**  
Add fabrication, installation / assembly, civil works, and electrical work as separate cost categories.

**Inputs**

- Labor item catalog
- Service item catalog
- Estimated days, hours, or packages
- Category-specific unit costs

**Output**

- Labor and service line items
- Labor / services subtotal

**Why it reduces repetitive manual work**
Reusable labor and service items prevent teams from manually rewriting common work packages like welding, installation, site preparation, or electrical work.

## 4. Add Logistics and Equipment

**Goal**  
Include delivery, handling, rentals, and tools required to execute the work.

**Inputs**

- Logistics items
- Equipment rental items
- Estimated trips, days, or packages

**Output**

- Logistics / equipment line items
- Logistics / equipment subtotal

**Why it reduces repetitive manual work**
These costs are easy to forget when quotes are built manually. Keeping them in the database makes the checklist more complete.

## 5. Apply Overhead and Contingency

**Goal**
Add internal operating cost and risk allowance before calculating the final commercial margin.

**Inputs**

- Direct cost subtotal
- Overhead percentage
- Contingency percentage

**Output**

- Overhead amount
- Contingency amount
- Adjusted cost base

**Why it reduces repetitive manual work**
Standard percentage inputs make the calculation repeatable and reduce the chance of missing overhead or risk allowance.

## 6. Apply Margin

**Goal**  
Apply the expected business margin to the adjusted cost base.

**Inputs**

- Direct costs
- Overhead
- Contingency
- Margin percentage

**Output**

- Margin amount
- Final estimated total

**Why it reduces repetitive manual work**
The margin calculation can be generated from structured quote data instead of being recreated manually in each spreadsheet.

## 7. Review Manually Before Sending to Client

**Goal**  
Confirm that the quotation is realistic, accurate, and ready for external sharing.

**Inputs**

- Draft quote
- Project assumptions
- Material, labor, service, logistics, and equipment line items
- Overhead, contingency, and margin values

**Output**

- Approved quotation
- Revision notes or delivery notes

**Why it reduces repetitive manual work**
Reviewers can focus on judgment, accuracy, and missing scope instead of rebuilding calculations from scratch.
